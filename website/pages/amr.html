AMR Hackathon 2018 (Heidelberg, Germany)

I was selected to participate in a 3-day Antimicrobial Resistance (AMR) Hackathon organized by EIT Health Germany, in Heidelberg (Germany). We were divided in teams of 6/7 elements that would analyze AMR status in a different EU country.
My group was asked to:

  1. Analyze guidelines and regulation in France;
  2. Verify which EU directives are implemented to tackle this issue;
  3. Find successful stories and disastrous events that took place in a local or national level;
  4. Think of an innovative approach to handle AMR.

At the end, a poster and a 1-slide presentation were presented to 5 judges from EIT Health organization.
Outputs from the hackathon sessions were made available to DG Santé and local and national authorities, therefore impacting policy and strategic approaches to deal with AMR in Europe.
Bursary awarded.
